About Bob Law

I write this in my own name, and tell you exactly what it is.

I'm Bob Law, and I work in ocean freight forwarding, based in the Pearl River Delta in South China. Cargomap is my personal page — somewhere I write down what I see on the ground in this trade. It is a personal brand, not a company: it doesn't contract your carriage or issue bills of lading, and everything here is my own view rather than any employer's.

You've probably met people in this trade who hide what they are — palming business cards at a trade show, almost reluctant to say the word "forwarder" out loud. I'd rather stand up straight, tell you plainly what I do and don't do, and let you make up your own mind. If I'm not the right fit, you'll know quickly — and that honesty is the whole reason this page exists.

The two lanes I follow most closely are China → Vietnam and China → Iraq. I write about them in depth because I actually know them — the ports, the paperwork, and the transshipment step where shipments quietly get stuck. Most of the value in this work is noticing a problem early and being straight with you about it.

The wider region — the Gulf, India, Southeast Asia — I follow through how cargo really routes and through working in the industry. On every page I try to be honest about where my firsthand experience ends and where I'd lean on people who know a particular port better than I do.

Outside work I photograph the places I walk through — nearly every image on this page is mine. I think the same habit of paying attention is what makes someone good at this work.

A plain note on what this is: these are personal notes from someone who works in freight, not a sales pitch and not legal or commercial advice. If we ever end up working together, that would be handled through a separate, formal agreement — and you'd always be reaching me directly, one person who'll give you a straight answer, even when the straight answer is "that's outside what I know."
